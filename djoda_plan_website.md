# Clinical Curator Copy Replacement Plan

## Summary
- Reposition the whole site around one clear promise: recover lost revenue from the patient base clinics already have.
- Keep the audience broad for healthcare practices and clinics.
- Use conservative proof: no invented network size, fake testimonials, stale dates, or lifetime-value-first claims.
- Keep the current layouts and routes, but reframe `/build-my-app` as a blueprint-session page, not a custom app page.

## Public Copy Changes
- Sitewide CTA: `Build my app` -> `Book a Blueprint`
- Footer brand: `Clinical Curator Excellence` -> `Clinical Curator`
- Do not publish demo numbers, named testimonials, or hard-coded dates unless they are verified and current.

## Test Plan
- Every page should lead with recovered appointments, recovered revenue, or reduced staff workload within the first screen.
- No page should position the product as a generic practice-management system, campaign app, or custom app build.
- No section should rely on lifetime value as the main ROI argument.
- Remove or replace all fake testimonials, inflated network claims, and stale placeholder dates.
- CTA language should be consistent across pages: `Book a Blueprint`.
- Any remaining numbers must be verified and current before publish.

## Assumptions
- Audience stays broad across healthcare practices and clinics.
- The current page layouts and route names remain unchanged.
- Proof stays conservative until real client metrics and testimonials are available.
- If verified proof becomes available later, it should replace the placeholder metric values, not the replacement labels and positioning above.
